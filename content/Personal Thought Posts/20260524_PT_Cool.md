Being cool is something that many people aspire to be. I’m not totally there myself yet, but working on it. I appreciate it a lot when I meet or see cool people doing what they do. I’ve found that the people I consider super cool all have a unique and slightly unconventional mannerism in how they do things. They do things differently, in their own way. Maybe *being cool is really just about living freely and expressing one’s self, with no hesitations or reservations (i.e. cool equals keeping it real)*. It’s funny how we’re often the most cool when we're just doing things without thinking anyone else is observing. The moment we notice there are people watching and observing, we abruptly cut it off and act “normal” again. Children don’t do this but as adults, we’re hyper aware when people are observing us or silently judging us. This takes us out of our flow and we start performing and doing things with an added layer that begins with the question: “What will people think?” I say stop worrying about what people think and just do what you do. Give yourself permission to do what you want. It doesn't really matter too much what people think, as long as you are doing something good with the intent to inspire or lead people to higher ideals. I say:

> "Being cool is about trying to become who you were always meant to be, regardless of what people may say or think. Become like an Oak tree that just keeps growing bigger, regardless of whether it’s sunny, rainy, cloudy, or snowy. The tree doesn’t break, bend, or worry about the weather. The tree just keeps doing what it does because it knows it's destined to be a great tree. It get's there by growing bigger and stronger everyday and in the process, the tree becomes a source of shade for other creatures.”
