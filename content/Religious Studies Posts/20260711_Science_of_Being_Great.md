I finished reading the book **The Science of Being Great** by the American author **[Wallace Wattles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallace_Wattles)** (1860 - 1911). Wallace Wattles was a prolific American author in the self-help genre who put out his best works in the early 20th century. This might be an early book in the self-help genre, which is a relatively recent phenomena because throughout past generations, people would often read history (because self-help books didn't exist) if they wanted to learn something new that would help them. This was a good book that I’m going to categorize as part of Religious Studies. Why? Because this book was really about how to be great not as the world defines it, but what true greatness is from a spiritual lens as defined by the Bible. As a practicing Christian, this is exactly the type of book I enjoy reading and learning from. I believe that we’ve perhaps lost sight of what it really means to be great in modern day 2026 and in our society overall. Maybe, just maybe, we are all chasing the wrong things and we are not as great as we think we are unless we align ourselves spiritually with the Creator. Wattles walks the reader over how to be great and breaks it down into an exact science, and I think the book is a fantastic one overall. I’m going to summarize the key points and save some quotes I want to remember for future reference.


> "Only God knows all truth; therefore only God can have real wisdom or the   
> right thing to do at all times, and man can receive wisdom from God."  

**A Person Cannot Become Truly Great Without Knowing Who God Is**: As I’ve gotten older, I went through a journey over many years to realize I don’t really care about achievements, status, money, material success, etc. too much anymore. Yes I need money to survive, but am I going to kill myself to become rich or define my success by my accomplishments or status? Nope, I don’t think so. But then society tells us, how could someone become great if they are not famous, or if they don’t accomplish something so grand that their name is etched in history? How could someone be great if they are not immensely rich and own an empire? How could someone become great if their co-workers and contemporaries are better than them? I’ve realized that the message we hear in society needs to be amended. Rather it’s become so evident to me that knowing who God is gives someone the real source of true greatness, with the wisdom to know what to do in all situations. Contrary to popular belief, true greatness is having a strong character, staying humble, and always doing the right thing. It’s about showing mercy, justice, integrity, living ethically, and most importantly: serving other people and not just living to satisfy our own wants and needs. These values and lessons we can only learn if we get closer to God, because they are not natural human responses in our daily lived experiences. Therefore as said best in Luke 9;48: “*For he who is least among you all - he is the greatest*”, where the “*least among you*” is describing someone who humbles him or herself and puts serving others as paramount. 

> "Nothing was ever in any man that is not in you; no man ever had more  
> spiritual or mental power than you can attain, or did greater things than  
> you can accomplish. You can become what you want to be."  

**Any Person Can Become Great**: The bottomline is, anyone can become “great” or become a “genius” if they desire to be. The only thing is, they have to do it in the right way and with the right methods. It doesn’t really matter what a person’s family background is, socio-economic status is, what they’ve done in the past, etc. when it comes to being a great person. If a person is aligned with the Creator, there is actually NO way that they CAN’T become great. Why? Because alignment allows someone to have the right spirit, which leads to the right mindset, which ultimately leads to the right actions. One has to adopt the mindset that one is a “*god among gods*”, meaning that a person is never superior to anyone else, but also never inferior to anyone else. Practically speaking, it means to respect everyone in their domains of expertise, but never believe that anyone is better than you because you also have your own domains of expertise. It’s not true that in life there are special people who are destined to do great things as *main characters*, while others are destined to be average and watch as *non-player characters* (NPCs). This becomes a certainty however if we don’t grow and seek new challenges. It’s important to learn from people that you admire, but never fall into the mental trap of putting them on a pedestal and thinking that they are much better than you. It’s not true, because one can become just as great in their own domains of expertise with enough practice and dedication.
